Status: ACTIVE (opened 2026-07-23 after M1 closure; loop runs through M3
per the standing push-through directive). Port the build to a pinned
TL 2026 snapshot — the project's first annual rebase, and its founding
differentiator (DESIGN §1: "current TeX Live"). `luahbtex` exits the

  TL 2026 artifacts: hello-world per engine (xetex, pdftex), one
  bibtex8 document, one makeindex document.

  TL 2026 — a build-side pin, not runtime code), demo smoke 4/4,
  runtime suite green with regenerated fixtures, license audit green.

  carries xetex, pdftex, bibtex8, xdvipdfmx, makeindex, kpsewhich.
- The rebase is captured as a scripted, documented operation (the
  DESIGN §6.2 `make rebase` seed — honest about what is still manual).
- M3's gates re-validate the rebased tree immediately after (recorded
  trade-off from the swap amendment).

## Risks / standing decisions

- **emsdk 3.1.43 vs TL 2026 C++.** Newer vendored harfbuzz/icu may
  need a newer clang/libc++. Decision rule in item 4; a bump cascades
  into M3's container re-pin (already anticipated by the swap
  amendment) and must not be silent.
- **Upstream never built TL 2026.** The busytex Makefile's TL build
  targets may not survive texk/ build-system reorg between 2023 and
  2026 — this is the expected patch-by-patch grind (PROMPT.md M1
  original text). Journal discipline is the deliverable as much as
  the build.
- **ISO availability.** TL 2026 must come from the TUG historic
  archive (frozen); if the 2026 ISO is not yet in historic/ (release
  year), pin the exact dated ISO from the release area with its
  published checksum and note the mirror-rotation exposure + a
  re-pin-at-M3 reminder.
- **LuaTeX removal scope.** Dropping luahbtex also drops lua53 and
  LuaJIT-adjacent deps from the wasm link — verify no other program
  links them (kpathsea tools don't); smaller wasm is expected and
  budget-noted, not silently absorbed.
- **Fixture churn.** Engine banners and log cosmetics WILL change;
  the parser/machine detectors are version-agnostic substrings — any
  detector that breaks is a real finding for the annual-rebase log.
